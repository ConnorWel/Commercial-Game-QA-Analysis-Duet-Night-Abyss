## 🐞 BUG-DNA-008: [VFX/State Machine] "Truffle and Filbert" high-speed skill VFX loops infinitely during cutscenes, extending BUG-DNA-004

**Reporter:** [Connor Wei]
**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Environment/Version:** Duet Night Abyss (Current Live/Beta Build)
**Module:** VFX / State Machine / Cinematic Interruption

### 1. Severity & Priority
* **Severity:** Medium - Highly distracting during narrative moments.
* **Priority:** P3

### 2. Pre-conditions
* Same pre-conditions as BUG-DNA-004.
* Player is approaching a cutscene trigger volume.

### 3. Steps to Reproduce
1. Enter Truffle and Filbert's Ultimate "Riding" state.
2. Activate the small skill (E) to trigger high-speed movement.
3. Collide with a cutscene/dialogue trigger volume while the skill is active.
4. Observe the character's feet/mount visually during the cinematic sequence.
5. Exit the cutscene.

### 4. Actual Result
When the cutscene takes over, the visual effects (VFX) and animation loops for the high-speed movement (specifically around the feet/mount) fail to terminate. They play continuously and infinitely over the character model during the entire narrative sequence. Once the cinematic ends, BUG-DNA-004 is immediately triggered, and the character auto-dashes without input.

**Video Evidence:** [Click here to watch the reproduction video](在此处替换为你的YouTube链接)

### 5. Expected Result
Entering a cinematic volume should fire an `OnInterrupt` event that forcefully clears all active ability logic, destroys temporary particle systems (VFX), and resets the Animator to an idle state.

### 6. Investigation Notes & Root Cause Hypothesis
**QA Analysis:**
This confirms the hypothesis from BUG-DNA-004. The cinematic controller is successfully overriding the character's `Transform` (position/rotation) for the cutscene, but it is completely ignoring the `Ability System` or `Action State Machine`. Because the `StopSkill()` function is bypassed, the particle emitters and audio sources attached to the skill remain active in the hierarchy.

## 🐞 BUG-DNA-009: [Level Design/Control] Player gets permanently stuck in terrain when resisting forced spline movement in "Taixu Mausoleum" quest

**Reporter:** [Connor Wei]
**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Environment/Version:** Duet Night Abyss (Current Live/Beta Build)
**Module:** Character Control / Level Design / Cinematic Camera

### 1. Severity & Priority
* **Severity:** High - Completely soft-locks the player, breaks the native "Unstuck" feature, and requires a full quest reset.
* **Priority:** P2

### 2. Pre-conditions
* Active Quest: "Storm Approaching - Deep into Taixu Mausoleum" (山雨欲来-深入太虚陵).
* Player is operating the character "Zhiliu" (止流).
* Player is approaching the final staircase.

### 3. Steps to Reproduce
1. Approach the final stairs to trigger the "Forced Walk/Cinematic Camera" sequence where the character is forced down the center of the path.
2. While being forced forward, hold `S` (backward) or combine it with `A/D` (strafing) to resist the forced movement right before reaching the exact entrance node.
3. Allow the character to deviate slightly and collide with the environmental obstacles on the sides of the path.
4. Attempt to move away using `WASD`.
5. Attempt to use the game's built-in "Unstuck/Reset Position" tool.

### 4. Actual Result
The character gets permanently embedded/stuck in the side obstacles. `WASD` inputs only rotate the character in place but yield zero displacement. Critically, the game's native "Unstuck" feature fails to teleport the player out. The only resolution is abandoning and restarting the entire quest instance.

**Video Evidence:** [Click here to watch the reproduction video](在此处替换为你的YouTube链接)

### 5. Expected Result
If forced movement is required, player input (`WASD`) should be entirely disabled, or invisible collision walls (blockers) should smoothly guide the collision capsule along the intended path without allowing snagging. The "Unstuck" feature should override any local state.

### 6. Investigation Notes & Root Cause Hypothesis
**QA Analysis:**
The forced walk is likely driven by a Spline Controller or a rigid NavMesh constraint. When the player inputs `S` or `A/D`, they apply opposing physical velocity, pushing the character's collision capsule outside the allowed spline boundary and into un-navigable geometry. 
* The "Unstuck" tool likely fails because the Spline Controller script is still active and constantly overriding the character's `Transform.Position`, nullifying the teleport command sent by the Unstuck UI.

## 🐞 BUG-DNA-011: [Quest/Scene] Bypassing central cutscene trigger allows premature portal use, causing permanent quest-state leak into overworld

**Reporter:** [Connor Wei]
**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Environment/Version:** Duet Night Abyss (Current Live/Beta Build)
**Module:** Quest Logic / Trigger Volumes / Scene Streaming

### 1. Severity & Priority
* **Severity:** High - Massive sequence break resulting in persistent world-state contamination.
* **Priority:** P1

### 2. Pre-conditions
* Active Quest: "Reforging Black and White - Arrive at the end of Taixu Mausoleum" (重铸黑白-抵达太虚陵尽头).

### 3. Steps to Reproduce
1. Progress the quest to the final area.
2. Intentionally avoid stepping into the center of the arena (which houses the invisible trigger volume for the story cutscene).
3. Walk around the perimeter and approach the final portal directly.
4. Interact with the portal (which lacks any collision blocks or state-checks).
5. Load into the destination ("Hall of Discipline" exterior).
6. Observe the NPCs present in the open world and attempt to teleport to other regions to clear the state.

### 4. Actual Result
The player is successfully teleported into the normal, multiplayer-enabled overworld. However, because the central cutscene was skipped, the previous quest state ("Hurry to the Hall of Discipline") fails to unload. 
* The live overworld becomes contaminated: Quest-specific NPCs remain spawned in the open world alongside normal NPCs.
* Teleporting to other regions does not refresh the state.
* The player can still see and interact with other multiplayer users perfectly, but their local client is rendering an aborted quest state.

**Video Evidence:** [Click here to watch the reproduction video](在此处替换为你的YouTube链接)

### 5. Expected Result
The portal should either be disabled/blocked via a collision wall until the central cutscene `OnComplete` flag is thrown, OR the central cutscene trigger volume should span the entire width of the room so it cannot be physically bypassed.

### 6. Investigation Notes & Root Cause Hypothesis
**QA Analysis:**
This is a critical Level Design / Logic oversight. The `Unload_QuestScene()` function is likely bound to the end of the central cutscene. By skipping the cutscene, the player jumps to a new scene via the portal, but the Scene Manager never receives the command to garbage-collect the temporary quest NPCs, leaving them orphaned in the persistent overworld hierarchy.

## 🐞 BUG-DNA-013: [Rendering/Lighting] Environment rendering turns pitch black upon restarting "Walk with Zhiliu" quest, fixable by changing in-game time

**Reporter:** [Connor Wei]
**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Environment/Version:** Duet Night Abyss (Current Live/Beta Build)
**Module:** Rendering / Global Illumination / Quest State

### 1. Severity & Priority
* **Severity:** High - Completely destroys environmental visibility, making gameplay almost impossible.
* **Priority:** P2

### 2. Pre-conditions
* Active Quest Phase: "Reforging Black and White - Walk with Zhiliu" (重铸黑白-陪止流走走).
* Location: Jiaolan Village (椒兰村).

### 3. Steps to Reproduce
1. Enter the Jiaolan Village quest area.
2. Intentionally walk outside the quest boundary to trigger the "Quest Failed, please restart" prompt.
3. Confirm the restart and load back into the quest.
4. Observe the environment rendering.
5. Open the game menu and use the in-game clock to skip/change the Time of Day.

### 4. Actual Result
Upon restarting the quest, the entire environment renders in an ink/pitch-black state. Skyboxes, directional lights, and ambient lighting appear broken or missing.
* **Workaround:** Using the in-game clock tool to change the time successfully forces the engine to recalculate the lighting, instantly fixing the black rendering and restoring the environment.

**Video Evidence:** [Click here to watch the reproduction video](在此处替换为你的YouTube链接)

### 5. Expected Result
The quest restart function should cleanly re-initialize all scene parameters, including the Global Illumination volume and active Directional Lights.

### 6. Investigation Notes & Root Cause Hypothesis
**QA Analysis:**
This is a Lighting Environment initialization failure tied to the Quest State Machine. 
When the quest fails, the game unloads the current scene lighting. When it rapidly reloads the player at the checkpoint, the script responsible for querying the server/client for the correct Time of Day (ToD) and injecting it into the `Skybox/Directional Light` parameters times out or fails to execute. Changing the time manually via the UI fires a global `UpdateLighting()` event, which successfully forces the engine to populate those missing rendering variables.
